Effect of the rhizome of Zingiber officinale on the growth of Wisconsin Fast Plants

Building a Site with Bootstrap

Background

The experiment aimed to determine if the ginger rhizome, when added to soil, can significantly increase the height of Wisconsin Fast Plants. It was hypothesized that the variety of nutrients such as antioxidants in ginger, in addition to compounds produced by any endophytic bacteria, would help the Wisconsin Fast Plants grow. The prediction was that plants in soil containing a higher ginger concentration would become taller than plants in soil with a lower ginger concentration. The experiment was conducted with 5 test levels: 14 plants were given no soil, 14 were given soil with no ginger, 14 were given soil with 20g of ginger, 14 were given soil with 40 g of ginger, and 14 were given soil with 80g of ginger. The height of each plant was collected once a week for 35 days, and the data was then graphed to look for trends. ANOVA and t-tests were also performed to determine which groups were significantly different from others. As the amount of ginger increased, the number of dead plants increased, but the heights of the living plants were greater than those of the control plants. The findings of the experiment were that small amounts of ginger boosted plant growth, but larger amounts change the composition of the soil too much, and create an environment that the plants are not capable of surviving in.
